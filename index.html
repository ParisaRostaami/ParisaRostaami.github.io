01 — About

I'm a PhD student in Computer Science & Electrical Engineering at Wichita State University, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the SoundMind Neurovision Innovation Lab on brain tumor segmentation.

My background brings together natural language processing, computer vision, and machine learning for healthcare. I'm drawn to problems where models need to be both accurate and interpretable — recently, building gender-aware explainable AI to detect Parkinson's disease from speech, which earned the First Place Paper Award at KDSC 2026.

Alongside research, I've taught and mentored across two countries — from undergraduate data-structures courses to graduate data-science capstones — and I genuinely enjoy helping people learn to code.
